# Emergency Shutdown Procedures

## Purpose

Defines standardized actions for safely stopping equipment during emergencies to protect personnel, assets, and the environment.

## Scope

Applies to all production machinery, utilities systems, and automated workcells.

## Shutdown Triggers

Emergency shutdowns may be initiated due to safety hazards, equipment malfunction, fire risk, or environmental incidents.

## Execution Steps

Operators follow predefined shutdown sequences prioritizing energy isolation, motion cessation, and hazard containment. Emergency stop devices are tested regularly.

## Post-Shutdown Actions

All shutdown events are documented, investigated, and reviewed to prevent recurrence and improve emergency preparedness.
